# MCM/ICM O-Award Writing Guide

This guide extracts writing patterns, academic vocabulary, and sentence templates from 6 Outstanding papers (2024 MCM/ICM Problems A-F).

---

## 1. Assumption Statement Patterns

### Template Structures
```
• Assumption [N]: We assume that [condition/behavior].
  Justification: [Reason based on domain knowledge/simplification need].
```

### Effective Phrasings
| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| "We assume that X is/does..." | "We assume that all lampreys were bred in the seventh year" |
| "X is assumed to be/have..." | "Weather at sea is assumed to be stable" |
| "We consider X as..." | "We consider the average water level as the initial value" |
| "X can be considered as..." | "Lake St. Clair can be considered as a distinctive river entity" |

### Justification Phrases
- "This is justified because..."
- "This assumption holds since..."
- "In reality, [fact], so to simplify the model we..."
- "Existing studies show that..."
- "Due to the difficulty in accurately determining X, we..."
- "Since [condition], it is reasonable to assume..."

## 2. Model Introduction Patterns

### Naming Your Model
Always give models distinctive names:
- "Discrete Population Dynamics Model Based on Age Grouping"
- "Leverage Quantitative Momentum Capture Model"
- "Demand-Risk Equilibrium Model for Insurance (DBMI)"
- "Client Triangle Profile Model"
- "Grid-Search Random Forest Swing Predictor Model (GSRF)"

### Model Introduction Templates
```
We establish/develop/construct a [Model Name] considering [key factors].
By utilizing [method/technique], this model [capability/function].
```

```
To [objective], we propose the use of [technique] to [action] more accurately.
Through this approach, we aim to [goal].
```

```
We introduce [concept] into the model, assuming that [condition].
The model takes into account [factor A] as well as [factor B].
```

### Explaining Why a Method Was Chosen
- "Given the uncertainty, complexity, and randomness of X, we propose..."
- "For the convenience of model establishment and subsequent analytical calculations..."
- "To streamline the problem and facilitate the simulation of real-life conditions..."
- "Due to the complexity of equation (X), it is difficult to obtain an analytical solution through mathematical methods. Therefore, we use..."

## 3. Mathematical Expression Patterns

### Introducing Equations
```
According to [principle/law/formula], we have:
   [equation]                                        (N)
where [variable] represents [meaning], and [variable] denotes [meaning].
```

```
The [quantity] can be expressed as follows:
   [equation]                                        (N)
Here, [variable] is the [description], and [variable] stands for [description].
```

```
We define [concept] as:
   [equation]                                        (N)
This [definition/formulation] indicates that [interpretation].
```

### Connecting Equations
- "By simultaneously combining equations (X), (Y), and (Z), we obtain..."
- "Substituting (X) into (Y) yields..."
- "According to relevant studies, [relationship]:"
- "When considering [additional factor], the above equation becomes..."
- "This can be aptly described using a [function type]"

## 4. Results Presentation Patterns

### Introducing Figures
- "The results are shown in Figure X."
- "As shown in Figure X, it is evident that..."
- "Figure X displays/illustrates/presents..."
- "From Figure X, we can see/observe that..."
- "The colored lines in Figure X indicate..."

### Describing Trends
- "exhibits/shows a [positive/negative] correlation with..."
- "increases/decreases as [variable] rises/falls"
- "reaches a maximum/minimum of [value] at [condition]"
- "fluctuates within a range of ±X%"
- "stabilizes at approximately [value] after [time/condition]"

### Quantitative Comparisons
- "Compared with [baseline], our model demonstrates improved performance in..."
- "The prediction accuracy reaches X%, with Y% improvement over..."
- "The degree of fluctuation is X% and Y% respectively when..."
- "The recovery time decreases from X to Y through..."

### Drawing Conclusions from Results
- "This suggests that..."
- "From this, we deduce/conclude that..."
- "Therefore, we can determine that..."
- "This indicates/shows/reveals that..."
- "Based on the above analysis, we find that..."

## 5. Limitation and Uncertainty Phrases

### Acknowledging Limitations
- "It is worth noting that..."
- "However, the improvement in X is not evident due to..."
- "This approach has somewhat constrained..."
- "Due to the difficulty in accurately determining X, initial approximations are provided..."
- "The model may not capture all the nuances of..."

### Expressing Uncertainty
- "may be influenced by..."
- "is subject to significant randomness"
- "exhibits varying degrees of..."
- "introduces uncertainty to the model"
- "poses a challenge in..."

### Hedging Language
- "It is anticipated that..."
- "This may indicate that..."
- "It can be postulated that..."
- "is likely to..."
- "is expected to..."

## 6. Academic Transition Words and Phrases

### Adding Information
- Furthermore, Moreover, Additionally, Besides, In addition
- "It is important to note that..."
- "It is worth noting that..."

### Contrasting
- However, Nevertheless, Conversely, On the other hand
- "While [A], [B] demonstrates..."
- "Despite [limitation], our model..."

### Cause and Effect
- Therefore, Consequently, Thus, As a result, Hence
- "This leads to..."
- "Due to [cause], [effect]"

### Sequencing
- Firstly, Secondly, Then, Subsequently, Finally
- "Following this, we..."
- "In the forthcoming sections, we will..."

### Summarizing
- In summary, To summarize, In conclusion, Overall
- "Based on the above analysis..."
- "Combining these factors..."

## 7. Figure and Table Caption Styles

### Figure Captions
```
Figure N: [Descriptive noun phrase]
```
Examples:
- "Figure 5: Current in Ionian Sea"
- "Figure 10: Validation of the Plausibility of the Female Percentage"
- "Figure 12: Confusion Matrix"

### Subfigure Labels
```
(a) [Description A]  (b) [Description B]
```

### Table Captions
```
Table N: [Noun phrase describing content]
```
Examples:
- "Table 1: Notation"
- "Table 2: Model Initial Parameter Values"
- "Table 4: Geographic information of the Great Lakes"

## 8. Sensitivity Analysis Writing

### Setup Description
```
To analyze the influence of [parameters], we focus on the effects of 
[param1], [param2], and [param3] on the model output when varied 
over a small range. We perturbed each parameter by ±X% to analyze 
[output variable].
```

### Results Description
```
From Figure X, we can see that changes in [parameter] caused the 
fluctuations to reach Y%, while the fluctuations caused by other 
parameters were within Z%. This shows that changes in [output] 
are very sensitive to [parameter], making it the most influential 
parameter.
```

### Robustness Statement
```
It can be observed that [output] is not sensitive to variations in 
[parameter] and remains relatively stable. This indicates the 
robustness of our model.
```

## 9. Strengths and Weaknesses Writing

### Strengths Format
```
Strengths:
• [We + verb phrase describing advantage]
  - "We make age grouping of populations, which can effectively..."
  - "Our model selects environmental factors comprehensively and scientifically"
  - "The control algorithm combines prediction module with feedback control"
```

### Weaknesses Format
```
Weaknesses:
• [Acknowledgment of limitation + potential impact]
  - "In reality, X exists, and our model does not take this into account, so..."
  - "The model may face challenges when applied to..."
  - "Stakeholders are not considered comprehensively enough"
```

## 10. Memo/Letter Writing Style

### Opening
```
Dear [Title/Organization],

We are writing to present an overview of our model/research/findings. 
[Brief context of the problem and its importance].
```

### Presenting Key Findings
```
Our analysis reveals that...
According to our model, [finding].
The results suggest that...
```

### Recommendations
```
Based on our findings, we recommend the following:
• [Specific actionable recommendation]
• [Second recommendation with rationale]

Implementing these measures can effectively [outcome].
```

### Closing
```
We sincerely hope that [aspiration related to findings].
We anticipate the opportunity to delve deeper into our model with you 
and address any inquiries or concerns you might have.

Yours Sincerely,
Team #[Number]
```

## 13. Avoiding Weak Phrases

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "very important" | "crucial", "critical", "essential" |
| "a lot of" | "substantial", "significant", "considerable" |
| "shows" (overused) | "demonstrates", "illustrates", "reveals" |
| "good results" | "satisfactory performance", "robust outcomes" |
| "big/small" | "substantial/negligible", "significant/minor" |
| "get" | "obtain", "derive", "acquire" |
| "thing" | [specific noun] |
